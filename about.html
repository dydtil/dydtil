Unique Story

It all began...

in early 2019. The Youth Observatory conceived the Creating Networks Project Competition and each of the co-founders was preselected as a participant for the competition.

After a seemingly unending search, the three of us found ourselves. From then on, we have joint our heads together, combined each of our skills and conceived the project which is now called DYDTIL. Research was carried out, networks were established, new relationships were formed, skills were assessed and many more activities undertaken so that we could arrive at this amazing project.
In order to achieve this, we have to make sure we get to those in need. That is a priority. To this effect, we sought the aid of every possible stakeholder: governments, non-governmental organisations, individuals, policy makers, civilians, politicians, people of influence and a host of many others. It was not an easy task, and there are still more hurdles ahead of us. Making others see from our point of view is daunting, but in order to achieve our goals, the most important which is centred around the growth and development of our trainees and learners, we have to disregard our individual pleasures and uphold hardwork. Thank you for walking with us!

  • Why Us?

Curbing youth unemployment through inclusive technopreneurship training and literacy, skill acquisition in ICT so they can be up to speed with the digital advancements in today's era.

Obviously, we are not the first of our kind. We are different in that we look for the youth in need and not the youth in need looking for us. We are not focused on making any profits for ourselves, but rather we are focused on ensuring our trainees and learners create sources of income for themselves and to develop their communities in the long run.
